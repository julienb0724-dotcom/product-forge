# Research Agenda: Emporia Energy Dashboard Redesign

## Brief
Redesign the Emporia Vue mobile app's energy dashboard. The current app shows
circuit-level energy monitoring, solar/battery flow diagrams, EV charger control,
and smart plug management. The redesign should transform it from a monitoring tool
into an AI-powered energy intelligence platform that speaks in dollars, not kilowatts.

## Target User
Tech-forward homeowners (25-55) in Colorado, Texas, and California who already have:
- Emporia Vue (circuit-level monitoring)
- Solar panels (with or without battery storage)
- EV charger (Emporia or third-party)
- Smart thermostat

## Key Questions for Research

### Market Validation
- How many US homes have solar + battery + EV? Growing how fast?
- What do homeowners actually pay for energy management software?
- Is there willingness to pay for AI optimization vs free monitoring?

### Competitive Intel
- How do Sense, Span, Tesla, and Enphase apps differ in UX approach?
- What's the Lunar Energy app doing differently with AI?
- Which competitors show cost/savings data prominently vs kW data?

### User Pain Confirmation
- What are the top complaints about the current Emporia app on Reddit?
- Do users actually look at circuit-level data daily, or just during setup?
- What causes users to stop opening energy monitoring apps after 30 days?

### Pricing Signals
- What's the price range for premium energy management subscriptions?
- Do users pay for rate plan optimization features?
- What's the VPP revenue potential per enrolled home?

### Regulatory Landscape
- Which states have active VPP programs for residential batteries?
- How do NEM 3.0 (CA) and similar policies change the value proposition?
- What utility partnerships exist for demand response programs?

## Research Priorities
1. User pain confirmation (are we solving the right problem?)
2. Competitive intel (what's the UX state of the art?)
3. Pricing signals (will people pay?)
4. Market validation (is the market big enough?)
